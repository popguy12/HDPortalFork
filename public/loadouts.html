About the loadouts

Hideous Destructor has three zones for player encumbrance, which affect fatigue/stamina and sprinting capability (you can see which zone you are in by the color of your encumbrance number: green, yellow, or red). Accordingly, the list on this page was split into three categories, sorted by how much bulk each loadout starts the player with.

Light loadouts (<480 bulk)

One Doomed Space Cowboy

by HexaDoken

You're gonna carry that weight. Might as well, because you don't have anything else in your pockets.

One Doomed Space Cowboy: rev, 355 44
71 blocks

Grunt

by HexaDoken

A more typically-geared foot soldier than the well-off stock loadouts. Better find someone else's medikit, because you weren't issued with one.

Grunt: z66 nogl 1, 450 3, awg, stm
194 blocks

Huntsman

by Caligari87

Since the Tyrant's invasion, Man is no longer the most dangerous game. A civilian sporting loadout focused on patience and precision.

Huntsman: bos 1, 7mm 50, lad 1
287 blocks

Lumberjack

by Caligari87

Yes, start with one 9mm mag in the pistol because you only brought it to shoot rattlesnakes with in the first place. Repack that sumbitch from the box as you go. May pick up shotgun only if found in field. A real flannel-wearin' man don't need any of them fancy-schmancy space-age peashooters.

Lumberjack: pis, saw - bat 4, med, 9mm 50, lad
146 blocks (299 with backpack)

Operator

by Caligari87

High-speed-low-drag take on the standard Rifleman loadout, emphasizing mobility and field procurement over full-kit "just in case" syndrome. Semi-auto rifle encourages shot placement instead of fire volume, cutting down on required ammo stores.

Operator: z66 semi 1, awg 1, frg 3, pis 1, 450 4, 915 2
330 blocks

Dante

by Caligari87

For when playing soldiers and security guards and lumberjacks is old hat and you just wanna be an emo demonic fuccboi with crazy gunslinging skills.

Variants: Cheat a soulsphere and some spiritual armor to really round out this stylish ensemble. Add a chainsaw and a battery ( cha, bat ) for extra melee goodness. Play without Blue Armor for the true Dante Must Die experience. Swap one autopistol for a revolver and some 9mm mags for .355 rounds if Nero is more your speed.

Dante: pis selectfire 1, pis selectfire 1, sla, awb, 915 12, shl 12
395 blocks

Dogfighter

by Eric

Combining high speed with powerful weapons, the Dogfighter can take out any single foe with ease. Use your rifle to discourage the rabble from interfering, and go Cyberdemon hunting with the Brontornis and Blooper's one-two punch. Swap out ZMs as they drop to conserve mags and avoid durability issues.

Dogfighter: z66 nogl 1, blo, bro, 450 1, rkt 1, brb 5, zrk 1, med 1, awg
400 blocks

Skirmisher

by Eric

Because going fast doesn't necessarily mean going small. Beware that unlike the Slayer, the Hunter is a bit long to fill the role of a sidearm properly. Consider switching it out if expecting Ninja Pirates.

Skirmisher: hun, lib nogl 1, 730 1, zrk 1, med 1, awg
408 blocks

Raider

by Caligari87

An optimized, extra-mobile variant of the Skirmisher boasting heavier armor and a backpack for extended operations in hostile territory, provided you keep the rifle stowed except when necessary. Zerk and extra stim optional, swap the battle armor for garrison armor if you want to carry another rifle magazine or reloading supplies.

Raider: hun, stm, awb, bak. lib nogl. 730 1. shl 20. med. zrk.
396 blocks

Turbo Killer

by Eric

A lightweight loadout equipped with the heaviest weapon available. For when you've got more enemies to kill than cover to hide behind.

Turbo Killer: vul, awb, zrk 2, med
415 blocks
