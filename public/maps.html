About the maps

Various maps that were either designed for HD, with HD in mind, or work really well with it as if they were designed for it.

Last updated: 05/07/21

Training maps

Hideous Destructor Bootcamp

by Various
Community highlight

The UAC Bootcamp. The center of training for all brave UAC cadets with hopes and dreams to keep their pure green Earth clean from the impending dangers of the Hell invasion, and also acts as training grounds for the Hideous Destructor Doom mod. These set of courses and obstacles are made so that beginners of Hideous Destructor may hopefully have a better time to train, adapt, and conquer the many complex mechanics and dangerous foes that are in the mod.

Custom Range

by Tartlman aka Bogus

Overhaul of Hideous Destructor's built in firing range that makes it far more complete. Every item is present, along with some new targets and practice areas. Includes a ballistics testing area which may be useful for modders, or people just looking to learn more about a certain gun.

Frag Range v1.0

by That0neBr075

Need to practice throwing and cooking grenades over various ranges? Then this map's for you.

Features 153 targets for you and your buddies to blow up. For a fun challenge, try seeing how many kills you can rack up using only 20 grenades. Certain targets might also trigger extra ordnance or special demons to spawn in, allowing for higher scores.

Everything else

Ashes 2063: Hideous Remaster

by SpecOps!
Community highlight

This update brings:

  • Completed Map 2;
  • Completely revamped areas and fights;
  • Non-Linear progression, multiple points to enter and exit. You choose where YOU want to go;
  • Wallbusting required to access certain secrets and sections;
  • You can now "Backtrack" between map 1 and 2;
  • Locations for merchants to spawn, IF you have the merchant mod enabled. Merchants remain in their locations even when changing maps, so you can always return to previous levels to buy gear! (Disclaimer: They may or may not spawn depending on your luck.)

Diabolus Ex

by Arvell/Arvy
Community highlight

Short but intricate and beautiful map inspired by Deus Ex. Technical gunfights in realistic spaces, and plenty of opportunity to avoid traps, approach fights from multiple directions, or attack enemies on your own terms (note that bullets can go through windows). The main sticking point is the mastermind fight, which mostly involves tediously peeking in and out of the elevator instead of using the open rooftop space.

Dimension of the Boomed

by Urthar

A surprisingly convincing set of Quake 1-styled maps. Along with some custom monsters borrowed from the source material, it also replaces most vanilla monsters without significant behavior changes. Here is a patch to overwrite them with HD monsters again. A rocket launcher is highly recommended. At the very least, pick one up before Map06.

Doom Recon

by Morcillete
Made for HD

Very large open areas suitable for realistic-range rifle battles, interspersed with small techbase-style buildings. Progression can be difficult due to sparse cover and tight chokepoints.

Heavy Cargo

by PapaSalmon
Made for HD

Techbase map with some low visibility outdoors sections and better lit indoor sections each at various different ranges, playable on singleplayer and multiplayer.

Mansion

by MrBoxBox
Made for HD

Short scenario map themed around infiltrating a mansion. Light enemy resistance, but various fun methods of approach.

Operation: Gambler

by YoBadMama
Made for HD

Plot: The UAC has finally tracked down the smuggling ring that has been selling advanced weaponry and captive demons to militia groups. You have been sent to the area through a series of tunnels that drops down into the complex. Your job is to eliminate the smugglers and reach the computer within their base to find out who is supplying them and to open the door in the courtyard for your escape.

Revolution!

by Thomas van der Velden

Lots of small-scale encounters in mostly realistic environments, well-suited for people new to HD. Much of the difficulty is frontloaded in the earlier maps, with maps 03 and 06 requiring particularly cautious play. There's just a couple problem encounters for the rest of the WAD after that, but nothing too serious. Use the tools you're given, look for secrets if you need to, and everything should be fine.
